[COLLECTION SCHEDULES & BIN SYSTEM]

THE 4-BIN COLLECTION SYSTEM

Red Lid Bin (Dark Green Body): General Waste/Rubbish
- Collection: Weekly
- Contents: Non-recyclable items, contaminated materials, items that won't decompose
- Capacity: Standard bin size

Yellow Lid Bin: Recycling
- Collection: Fortnightly (alternates with Purple lid)
- Contents: Paper, cardboard, glass, metal, hard plastics
- Capacity: Standard bin size

Purple Lid Bin: Glass Recycling
- Collection: Fortnightly (alternates with Yellow lid)
- Contents: Glass bottles and jars
- Purpose: Separates glass to improve recycling outcomes

Lime Green Lid Bin: FOGO - Food Organics Garden Organics
- Collection: Weekly
- Contents: Food scraps, garden waste, food-soiled materials
- Purpose: Diverts organic waste from landfill to composting

GENERAL RULES (All Collections)
1. Bin Placement: Place bins out the night before collection day
2. Bin Retrieval: Remove bins within 24 hours of empty (or when no longer in use that day)
3. Bin Spacing: Space bins 0.5 metres apart when presenting for collection
4. Handle Direction: Position bin handles facing towards your home
5. Weight/Size: Do not overfill bins; lids must close properly
6. Non-compliant bins: May not be collected if rules not followed

[FOGO BIN - FOOD ORGANICS GARDEN ORGANICS]

What Goes in FOGO Bins:
- Food scraps including meat, fish, bones, dairy, grains, fruit and vegetables
- Garden organics including grass, leaves, branches, garden prunings
- Paper and cardboard contaminated with food
- Food-soiled takeaway containers
- Compostable packaging

What Does NOT Go in FOGO:
- Plastic bags or plastic liners
- Non-food items
- Contaminated non-compostable materials
- Medical waste
- Pet waste

Collection: Weekly with red lid general waste bin

[YELLOW & PURPLE RECYCLING BINS]

Yellow Lid Recycling Contents:
- Paper and cardboard (newspapers, magazines, office paper, boxes)
- Cardboard: Flatten large boxes to fit in bin
- Glass bottles and jars (rinsed)
- Metal containers (tins, cans, aluminium, steel)
- Plastic bottles and hard plastic containers #1-#7 (rinsed)
- Cartons (juice, milk cartons)

What Does NOT Go in Recycling:
- Plastic bags, plastic wrap, plastic films
- Polystyrene or foam
- Kitchen items (plates, bowls)
- Contaminated or greasy materials
- Non-recyclable plastics

Purple Lid Glass Recycling:
- Glass bottles and jars only
- Must be rinsed
- Keep separate from general recycling
- Reduces contamination in recycling stream

Collection: Fortnightly (alternates week with Yellow lid)

[RED LID GENERAL WASTE]

General Waste Contents:
- Non-recyclable items (plastic film, polystyrene, contaminated paper)
- Items that won't decompose (ceramics, broken glass mixed with other items)
- Worn-out clothing and textiles
- Small hard waste items
- Broken goods that cannot be recycled

What Does NOT Go in General Waste:
- Recyclable materials (should go in yellow/purple bins)
- Food or garden waste (should go in FOGO bin)
- Large items (should use hard rubbish collection)
- Hazardous materials (batteries, chemicals, etc.)

Collection: Weekly

[HARD RUBBISH COLLECTION]

Maximum Collections:
- Residents can request hard rubbish collection maximum 2 times per calendar year
- Must be booked online with Council

What is Accepted:
- Large furniture (sofas, chairs, tables, beds)

- Wooden items (doors, floorboards, timber)
- Mattresses and cushions
- Garden equipment (dead trees, large branches)
- Broken items too large for bins

What is NOT Accepted:
- Hazardous materials (asbestos, chemicals, electronics with circuit boards)

[PLANNING OVERVIEW]

What is a Planning Permit?
A planning permit is formal approval from Council to use land in a particular way or construct buildings. Some uses require planning approval; others don't. Permits ensure development is appropriate for the location, respects the heritage character, and meets standards.

When Do You Need a Planning Permit?
Common cases requiring permits:
- Heritage changes (most common in Yarra)
- Demolition
- New construction or extensions
- Subdividing land
- Changing the use of land or buildings
- Fencing
- Tree removal
- Decks and outdoor structures
- Renovation and alterations
- Solar panels
- Signage

Important Note (July 2025 Change):
- As of 1 July 2025: Planning permit no longer required for liquor licensing applications
- LCV (Liquor and Gambling Victorian) licence still required - this is state licensing, not Council approval
- Other planning requirements still apply to the premises

Cases that typically DON'T require planning permits:
- Internal renovations not changing building structure
- Painting and minor maintenance
- Some minor garden works
- Maintenance of existing structures

CRITICAL: If in a heritage area, almost all exterior changes require permits.

IMPORTANT: If you're unsure, ask Council before spending time and money on plans. It's always better to check first.

[HERITAGE OVERLAY - CRITICAL FOR YARRA]

Heritage Protection in Yarra:
Yarra has extensive heritage overlay protection covering:
- Historic buildings
- Streetscape character
- Culturally significant sites
- Areas of architectural importance
- Precinct-wide protections

If Your Property Has Heritage Overlay:
- External changes likely require planning permit
- Interior changes usually not controlled
- Demolition not permitted without approval
- Tree removal may require approval
- Fencing changes require consideration
- Alterations must respect heritage character

Check Your Property:

[PLANNING APPLICATION STEP-BY-STEP PROCESS]

Step 1: Research Your Property
Before preparing plans, understand your property constraints:
- Check property title: Obtain from LANDATA or your conveyancer
- Check zoning: Use VicPlan at vicplan.delwp.vic.gov.au
- Check overlays: Look for Heritage, Environmental Audit, Inundation, Special Building
- Check for covenants: Listed on title; not administered by Council
- Check restrictions: Any encumbrances affecting the property
- Understand relevant planning scheme provisions

Essential Tools:
- Title search (LANDATA.com.au)
- VicPlan (vicplan.delwp.vic.gov.au) for zoning and overlays
- Yarra Planning Scheme: Available on Council website

Step 2: Get Professional Advice
For most applications, professional help is valuable:
- Architect for houses and complex designs
- Planning consultant for complex applications
- Heritage consultant for heritage area projects
- Town planner for land use questions
- Surveyor for boundary and subdivision work

Why: Professionals know what Council looks for and can increase approval chances and speed processing. Especially important in Yarra's heritage areas.

Step 3: Pre-Application Meeting (Recommended)
- Contact Council to request pre-application discussion
- Discuss proposal informally before formal application
- Get feedback on likely issues
- Clarify requirements
- Meet with planning officer
- Saves time and rework later

Contact: planningadmin@yarracity.vic.gov.au or 03 9205 5555
Response: Usually within 5 business days for appointment

Step 4: Site Analysis
Document your property thoroughly:
- Photographs of existing buildings and site
- Measurements and dimensions
- Existing vegetation and trees
- Views and solar access
- Drainage patterns
- Access points and driveways
- Heritage character and streetscape
- Neighboring properties and impacts

Step 5: Prepare Plans
Council requires specific plans depending on application type. Standard requirements:
- Site Plan: Shows property boundaries, building locations, access, parking
- Floor Plans: Interior layout showing room names and dimensions
- Elevations: Drawings showing building heights and appearance from different angles
- Cross-sections: Show ground levels, building heights
- Heritage Assessment (if applicable): For projects in heritage areas
- Design Statement: Explaining how proposal meets planning requirements

Plans must be to scale and clearly labeled. Sketch plans rarely acceptable for full applications.

Step 6: Submit via Approved Portal
- Create account at portal (Yarra eServices or VicSmart as applicable)
- Complete all required fields
- Attach all required documents as PDFs
- Pay application fee (check Council Fees & Charges page)
- Submit and retain reference number
- Receive confirmation email

Processing starts when payment is received.

- Provides lifelong identification if lost
- Available from veterinary clinics

- National database links pet to owner

Desexing - Mandatory:
- Required for all registered dogs and cats
- Veterinary surgical procedure

- Reduces population issues

Council encourages keeping cats indoors, particularly 7pm to 7am, to:

- Reduce cat injuries and disease

Wildlife Protection:

- Community responsibility to protect native species
- Indoor cats have longer, healthier lives
- No outdoor risks (cars, disease, fights)

Housing Requirements:
- Secure enclosure or catio recommended
- Safe outdoor spaces (enclosed patios)
- Indoor enrichment essential
- Veterinary care important

[ALCOHOL IN PUBLIC PLACES]

Alcohol-Free Zones:
Alcohol is prohibited in the following public spaces:

1. All Parks: 9pm to 9am daily
   - No alcohol in any public park
   - Year-round restriction
   - Applies to all beverages containing alcohol

2. Footpaths: Next to open licensed venues
   - Immediately adjacent to bars, clubs, pubs
   - Prevents glass and litter issues
   - Safety for patrons

3. Family Facility Entrances: Within 10m
   - Includes schools, libraries, playgrounds
   - Protects children and families
   - Buffer zone from alcohol venues

4. Roads and Vehicles: No alcohol on roads or in vehicles
   - Public consumption prohibited
   - Vehicle drinking prohibited (open container)

Special Alcohol-Free Zones:

Swan Street (Grand Final Weekend):
- AFL Grand Final weekend restrictions
- Prevents excessive street drinking
- Community safety measure
- Specific dates each year

New Year's Eve:
- All public places alcohol-free from 9pm Dec 30 to 9am Jan 1
- Protects safety during celebrations
- Prevents street disorder
- Community health focus

Penalties:
- Fines for alcohol possession in restricted zones
- Confiscation of beverages
- Potential further action for repeat offences
- Educational approach with first offenders

[OPEN AIR BURNING & FIRE SAFETY]

Prohibited Without Permit:
Open air burning is an offence unless you have a permit from Council:
- Fires in open air
- Burning grass, leaves, wood
- Rubbish burning
- Controlled fires

Exemptions (No Permit Required):
- BBQs and outdoor cooking
- Pizza ovens in private yards (certain conditions)
- Chimineas (portable fire containers, certain conditions)
- Licensed entertainment uses

Permit Process:
- Apply to Council for burning permit
- Specify: Type of material, quantity, date/time
- Council assesses: Wind, drought, fire danger period
- Permit issued if safe to proceed
- Conditions may apply

Fire Danger Period:
- October to May (dates vary by year)
- Restrictions more stringent during fire danger season
- Council declarations observed

[FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS - COMMON INQUIRIES]

BINS & WASTE:
Q: What day is my bin collection?
A: Bin day varies by location. The 4-bin system includes: Red (weekly general waste), Yellow (fortnightly recycling), Purple (fortnightly glass), Lime green (weekly FOGO). Check your collection day on the back of your rates notice or by phoning 03 9205 5555.

Q: What goes in the purple bin?
A: Glass bottles and jars only. No other items. Purple bin is collected fortnightly alternating with the yellow recycling bin.

Q: What goes in the FOGO bin?
A: Food scraps (meat, fish, dairy, vegetables) and garden waste (grass, leaves, branches). No plastic bags or non-compostable liners.

Q: How do I report a missed bin?
A: Report online via Council website, or phone 03 9205 5555. Provide your address, bin colour, and collection date. Usually attended within 2-3 working days.

Q: How often can I use hard rubbish collection?
A: Maximum 2 times per calendar year. Must book online through Council website.

PARKING:
Q: How do I pay a parking fine?
A: Pay online (preferred, 24/7), in person at customer service centres (Mon-Fri 8:30am-5pm), by phone 1300 792 772, by mail, or by BPAY (biller code 171074).

Q: How do I appeal a parking fine?
A: Appeal online via Council website using your reference number, explain your grounds, and attach evidence. You can also appeal by mail or phone.

Q: What is the payment plan for fines?
A: Once-off one-month extension available. If you hold a Pensioner Concession Card, DVA card, or Health Care Card, you can arrange a 3-month extension. Contact Council before penalty reminder is issued.

Q: How do I get a residential parking permit?
A: Apply online via Council website or in person at customer service centres. Provide proof of residence. Permits are allocated based on zone eligibility.

RATES:
Q: How do I pay my rates?
A: Multiple options: Online (credit card/direct transfer), BPAY (code 171074), Phone 1300 792 772, direct debit, in person, by mail, or Australia Post.

Q: What are the quarterly payment due dates?
A: 30 September, 30 November, 28 February, 31 May. Or pay in full by the first instalment date.

Q: Am I eligible for a pensioner rebate?
A: Yes, if you hold a Victorian Pensioner Concession Card or Department of Veterans' Affairs (DVA) Card. Apply in person at customer service centres with your card.

Q: I'm struggling to pay - what help is available?
A: Contact Council on 03 9205 5555 and speak to the Financial Hardship Officer. Council can discuss payment plans, deferrals, and support options.

PLANNING:
Q: Do I need a planning permit?
A: If you're in a heritage area (most of Yarra), almost any exterior change needs a permit. Check your property on VicPlan or contact Council. Pre-application advice available.

Q: How long does planning assessment take?
A: Varies by project complexity. Simple applications may be assessed within 28 days. Complex heritage projects may take 2-3 months. You'll be notified of progress.

Q: My property is in a heritage area - what does that mean?
A: Heritage overlay protects historic buildings. Most exterior changes require planning approval. Interior changes usually unrestricted. Get heritage advice before applying.

Q: Can I appeal a planning decision?
A: Yes. Decisions can be appealed to VCAT within 28 days. Information provided with Council decision letter.

Q: As of 1 July 2025, do I still need a planning permit for a liquor licence?
A: No. Planning permit no longer required for liquor licensing. But LCV (Liquor and Gambling Victorian) licence from state government is still required.

PETS:
Q: How do I register my dog or cat?
A: Register online (fastest), in person at customer service centres, by mail, or phone. Provide microchip number, proof of desexing, and vaccination proof.

Q: What are the maximum animals I can keep?
A: Dogs: Maximum 2 in a house, 1 in a unit/flat. Cats: Maximum 2 in a house, 1 in a unit/flat. Excess animal permit available if you want more.

Q: Is microchipping mandatory?
A: Yes. All dogs and cats must be microchipped before registration. Cost approximately $50-80 through vet.

Q: What do I do if I find a lost pet?
A: Contact Council on 03 9205 5555 or email info@yarracity.vic.gov.au. Check for tags or microchip at vet. Council holds found pets for 8-day reclaim period.

FAMILY SERVICES:
Q: How do I book an MCH appointment?
A: Call MCH Booking Line: 03 9205 5599. Or call 24/7 MCH Helpline: 132229. Eight MCH centres across Yarra.

Q: Is immunisation free?
A: Yes. Free drop-in immunisation clinics at multiple locations. No appointment needed.

Q: How do I enroll in childcare?
A: Contact individual centres for availability. Check with Council for service directory. Child Care Subsidy (CCS) available to eligible families.

LIBRARIES:
Q: How do I get a library card?
A: Join for free at any library branch. Can register online or in person. Immediate access to physical and digital collections.

Q: What programs does the library offer?
A: Book groups, author talks, children's programs, digital skills, gaming consoles, iPad lending (55+/disability), community pantry, and more.

NOISE:
Q: How do I report a noise complaint?
A: Report online (preferred), phone 03 9205 5555, or email info@yarracity.vic.gov.au. Document the problem: date, time, duration, type of noise. Keep a log for 7 days.

Q: What are building works hours restrictions?
A: Building works must comply with Clause 49 of General Local Law. Weekday and weekend hour restrictions apply. Check with Council for specific times.
